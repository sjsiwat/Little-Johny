# Johny OS

## Register

product

## Users

Single user — the builder and owner of this workspace. Uses it daily in Thai on desktop. Manages tasks, captures ideas, tracks spending, and plans the day from one place. Not an occasional visitor; this is a personal command centre.

## Product Purpose

A personal second brain that lives entirely in the browser. Zero friction for capture: task, note, expense, or natural-language command — one action to record, always available offline. Extends over time to connect LINE, AI, and cloud sync as satellites around a local-first core.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Warm · Human

Fast and capable like Linear or Superhuman — but unmistakably personal. The cat mascot, Caveat hand-lettering, and earthy palette signal a tool built by a person for themselves, not a product optimised for a demo.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — Notion clones, template UIs, hollow card grids, blue-grey palettes, hero-metric "Big Number · small label" blocks.
- **Corporate productivity tools** — rigid, cold, impersonal. No serif enterprise chrome, no dark-navy-and-gold fintech skin.
- **AI scaffolding aesthetics** — cream/sand body backgrounds (the 2026 AI default), uppercase eyebrow labels on every section, numbered 01 / 02 / 03 scaffolding as decoration rather than sequence.

## Design Principles

1. **Sharp capture, soft presence.** Every action must be one step. The UI disappears into the task — motion and decoration earn their place or get cut.
2. **Crafted, not templated.** The hand-drawn aesthetic (Caveat, offset sketch-shadows, cat mascot) is the brand, not a stylistic layer. Protect it; never normalise it toward generic productivity chrome.
3. **Thai first, bilingual always.** Thai is the primary language of data and headings. English is the system language. Both must be legible at every breakpoint.
4. **State over decoration.** Every colour, motion, and badge must convey real state — priority, completion, urgency, amount. Decoration that signals nothing gets removed.
5. **Local-first, extensible later.** Data lives in the browser first. The design should make the local experience feel complete, not provisional or "beta."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort. No formal compliance target. Always honour `prefers-reduced-motion`. Maintain readable contrast (≥4.5:1 for body text) in both light and dark themes. Thai body text must be legible at 15px on low-DPI screens.
